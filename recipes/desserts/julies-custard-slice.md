# Julie's Custard Slice

## Ingredients

### Pastry/Biscuit Layers

- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons baking powder (or 2 cups self-raising flour)
- Pinch of salt
- ¼ cup granulated sugar
- 60g unsalted butter, softened
- 1 large egg
- ½ cup milk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

### Custard Filling

- 1 litre (4 cups) whole milk
- 4 tablespoons (level) custard powder
- ¼ cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
- ½ can (approximately 170g) sweetened condensed milk (adjust to taste)

## Method

### Preparing the Pastry Layers

1. Preheat oven to 175°C. Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
2. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
3. Cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
4. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
5. Gradually add the dry ingredients alternating with the milk. Mix until just combined — do not over-mix.
6. Divide the dough into 5-6 equal portions. Roll each into a thin round cake, approximately 20-22cm diameter.
7. Prick pastry with a fork to prevent puffing. Bake for 10-15 minutes or until lightly golden.
8. Let the layers cool completely on a wire rack.

### Preparing the Custard Filling

1. Heat the milk in a saucepan over medium heat until just simmering.
2. In a separate bowl, mix the custard powder with the sugar and a small amount of warm milk to form a smooth paste.
3. Gradually pour the custard mixture into the simmering milk, stirring constantly.
4. Continue stirring over medium-low heat until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
5. Remove from heat and stir in the condensed milk. Adjust sweetness to preference.
6. Allow to cool slightly, stirring occasionally to prevent a skin forming.

### Assembling

1. Place one baked pastry layer on a flat surface. Spread a generous layer of custard over it.
2. Place another pastry layer on top. Repeat until all layers and custard are used, finishing with custard on top.
3. Crush any extra pastry and sprinkle over the top as decoration.
4. Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours (preferably overnight) to allow custard to set and layers to soften.
5. Cut into squares and serve.

## Notes

- A Julie (mom's) classic — the kind of dessert that disappears at family functions
- Optional: split the custard into 3 batches and colour each differently for a rainbow effect
- Dust with powdered sugar or drizzle with thin icing before serving
- The layers will soften as they absorb the custard — this is the point
